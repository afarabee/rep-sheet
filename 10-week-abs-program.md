# 10-Week Six-Pack Program
### Hip Flexor Safe · A/B Split · 5 Days/Week

**Schedule:** Alternate Workout A and Workout B across Monday–Friday.
Week 1 = A B A B A · Week 2 = B A B A B · Repeat pattern.

**Rest between sets:** Phase 1 = 30s · Phase 2 = 45s · Phase 3 = 60s

**Key rule:** Every exercise keeps feet planted or back on the floor. Zero hip flexor loading.

---

## Phase 1 — Weeks 1–3: Volume & Endurance
~12 minutes per session · Bodyweight only (band for Pallof)

### Workout A — Anti-Extension & Anti-Rotation

| Exercise | Sets × Reps/Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| RKC Plank | 3 × 30s | Squeeze glutes and quads hard, fists together, pull elbows toward toes. Way harder than a regular plank. |
| Dead Bug | 3 × 8 each side | Slow and controlled. Exhale as you extend. Low back stays glued to floor. |
| Pallof Press (band) | 3 × 10 each side | Anchor band at chest height on squat rack. Press out, hold 2s, return. Don't let it rotate you. |

### Workout B — Flexion & Anti-Lateral Flexion

| Exercise | Sets × Reps/Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| McGill Curl-Up | 3 × 12 | One knee bent, one leg straight. Hands under low back. Lift only head and shoulders — tiny range of motion. Alternate the straight leg each set. |
| Side Plank | 3 × 25s each side | Stack feet or stagger them. Squeeze bottom oblique. Keep hips up and forward. |
| Suitcase Carry | 3 × 40 steps (20 each side) | 15 lb kettlebell. Walk slow, stand tall, don't lean. Switch hands at 20 steps. |

### Phase 1 Progression
- **Week 1:** As written
- **Week 2:** Add 1 rep per set (or 5s to holds). Pallof hold becomes 3s.
- **Week 3:** Add another rep per set (or 5s to holds). Dead bug add 1s pause at full extension.

---

## Phase 2 — Weeks 4–6: Load Introduction
~15 minutes per session · Adding plates, dumbbells, and heavier bands

### Workout A — Loaded Anti-Extension & Anti-Rotation

| Exercise | Sets × Reps/Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Weighted Plank | 3 × 30s | Have your husband place a 10 lb plate on your upper back (between shoulder blades). RKC style — squeeze everything. |
| Weighted Dead Bug | 3 × 8 each side | Hold a 5 lb dumbbell in each hand. Arms go from ceiling to overhead as legs extend. Slow tempo. |
| Pallof Press + Step | 3 × 8 each side | Use a heavier band. Press out, take one lateral step away from anchor (increases tension), step back, return. |

### Workout B — Loaded Flexion & Anti-Lateral Flexion

| Exercise | Sets × Reps/Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Plate Crunch | 3 × 15 | Hold a 10 lb plate on your chest. Feet flat, knees bent. Crunch up squeezing ribs to pelvis. Slow negative (3s down). |
| Side Plank + Hip Dip | 3 × 10 dips each side | Drop hip to just above the floor, drive back up. Control the movement. |
| Suitcase Carry | 3 × 40 steps (20 each side) | Move up to a 25–30 lb dumbbell. Slow, tall, no lean. |

### Phase 2 Progression
- **Week 4:** As written
- **Week 5:** Weighted plank → 25 lb plate. Plate crunch → 25 lb plate. Dead bug → 8 lb or 10 lb dumbbells.
- **Week 6:** Add 1 set to each exercise (4 sets total). Suitcase carry → 35 lb dumbbell.

---

## Phase 3 — Weeks 7–10: Heavy Weighted Work
15–20 minutes per session · Progressive overload, higher intensity

### Workout A — Heavy Anti-Extension & Anti-Rotation

| Exercise | Sets × Reps/Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Weighted Plank | 4 × 35s | 35–45 lb plate on upper back. If 45 is too much, stay at 35 and add 5s each week. |
| Weighted Dead Bug | 4 × 6 each side | 10–15 lb dumbbells. Add a 2s pause at full extension. If low back lifts, drop the weight. |
| Pallof Walkout | 4 × 6 each side | Press out fully, then take 3 slow steps away from anchor. Hold 3s at max tension. Walk back. Reset. Use your heaviest band. |

### Workout B — Heavy Flexion & Anti-Lateral Flexion

| Exercise | Sets × Reps/Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Plate Crunch | 4 × 12 | 25–45 lb plate. Hug it to your chest. Slow 3s negative on every rep. If you're at 45 and it's easy, slow the negative to 5s instead of adding weight. |
| Side Plank Row | 4 × 8 each side | Side plank position, 15–20 lb dumbbell in top hand on the floor. Row it to your hip while holding the plank. Anti-lateral flexion under load. |
| Heavy Suitcase Carry | 4 × 40 steps (20 each side) | 40–50 lb dumbbell. Crush the grip. Tall spine. Slow walk. |

### Phase 3 Progression
- **Week 7:** As written (start conservative with weights — find your numbers)
- **Week 8:** Add 5–10 lbs to each loaded exercise OR add 5s to holds
- **Week 9:** Add 5–10 lbs again OR slow all tempos by 1s
- **Week 10:** Peak week. Heaviest loads. Max intent on every rep. You've earned it.

## Notes

- **Breathing:** Exhale on exertion (the crunch, the press, the hard part). Inhale on the return. On planks, breathe steadily through your nose — don't hold your breath.
- **If something bugs your hip flexors:** Stop that exercise immediately. Swap in an extra set of another exercise from the same workout day. Trust your body on this.
- **Warm-up:** 2 minutes of gentle movement before starting (walk around the garage, bodyweight squats, cat-cow stretches). Nothing fancy.
- **Ab soreness:** You'll be sore the first week. That's fine. By Week 2 your body will adapt to the frequency. If you're still wrecked by Day 4 of any week, take that day as active recovery (just Suitcase Carries, easy pace).
